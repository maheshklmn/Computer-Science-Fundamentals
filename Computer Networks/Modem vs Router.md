# Difference Between Modem and Router

| Feature | **Modem** | **Router** |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Purpose** | Connects your home network to the **Internet Service Provider (ISP)**. | Distributes internet from the modem to **multiple devices**. |
| **Function** | Converts analog signals (from ISP) into digital data and vice versa. | Routes data between devices and manages network traffic. |
| **Connection** | Connects directly to the ISP line (DSL/Cable/Fiber). | Connects to the modem and then to devices (wired or wireless). |
| **Network Range** | Does **not** provide Wi-Fi; supports **one device**. | Provides **Wi-Fi** and supports **multiple devices**. |
| **IP Address** | Receives **public IP address** from ISP. | Assigns **local IP addresses** (e.g., 192.168.x.x) to devices. |
| **Ports** | Usually has **1 LAN port**. | Has **multiple LAN ports** + Wi-Fi antennas. |

---

### Simple Understanding
- **Modem** brings internet *into* your home.
- **Router** spreads that internet *within* your home.
